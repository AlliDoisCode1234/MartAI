## Best Practices for AI Content

### 1. Start with a Strong Brief

The quality of AI output depends on your input:

```
Good prompt:
"Write a 1500-word guide on keyword research for
B2B SaaS marketers who are new to SEO. Use a
professional but approachable tone. Include
actionable steps and real examples."

Bad prompt:
"Write about keyword research"
```

### 2. Layer in Expertise

AI can't provide genuine expertise. Add:

- **Original research** - Your data and findings
- **Case studies** - Real examples from your work
- **Expert opinions** - Quotes from team members
- **Unique insights** - Perspectives only you have

### 3. Maintain Brand Voice

Train AI with your voice:

1. Provide style guide excerpts
2. Include sample paragraphs
3. Specify words to use/avoid
4. Define tone characteristics

### 4. Fact-Check Everything

AI hallucinates. Always verify:

- [ ] Statistics and numbers
- [ ] Dates and timelines
- [ ] Product features
- [ ] Person names and quotes
- [ ] Technical accuracy

> **Warning**: Publishing AI hallucinations damages E-E-A-T and can create legal issues.

### 5. Add Structure and Value

AI defaults to walls of text. Improve with:

- Clear heading hierarchy (H2, H3)
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Tables for comparisons
- Key takeaway boxes
- Visual breaks

### 6. Optimize for SEO

AI often misses SEO basics:

- Natural keyword integration
- Meta title/description quality
- Internal linking opportunities
- Featured snippet formatting
- Proper heading structure

## AI + Human Workflow

### The Optimal Process

```
1. Human: Create detailed brief
2. AI: Generate first draft
3. Human: Review for accuracy
4. Human: Add expertise/examples
5. Human: Refine for brand voice
6. Human: SEO optimization
7. Human: Final review
```

| Task          | Before AI | With AI | Savings |

| First draft   | 4 hours   | 30 min  | 87%     |

The time saved is reinvested in quality, not eliminated.

## Red Flags to Avoid

### 1. Generic Statements

**Bad**: "Content marketing is important for businesses."

**Better**: "Content marketing generates 3x more leads per dollar spent than paid search, according to DemandMetric."

### 2. Obvious Filler

**Bad**: "There are many factors to consider when..."

**Better**: "The three critical factors are: [specific list]"

### 3. Over-Hedging

**Bad**: "It might be possible that perhaps..."

**Better**: "Research suggests..." or "In our experience..."

## E-E-A-T and AI Content

Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) matters more with AI:

### Demonstrate Experience

- Include personal anecdotes
- Reference your own data
- Show what you've tried

### Show Expertise

- Author bylines with credentials
- Cite authoritative sources
- Technical accuracy

### Build Authority

- Consistent publishing
- Industry recognition
- Quality backlinks

### Ensure Trust

- Accurate information
- Clear sourcing
- Transparent about AI use

## Tools and Techniques

### Prompt Engineering

Use structured prompts:

1. **Role**: "Act as an SEO expert..."
2. **Context**: "Writing for marketers who..."
3. **Task**: "Create a guide that..."
4. **Format**: "Include headings, lists..."
5. **Constraints**: "Avoid jargon, stay under..."

### Quality Checklist

Before publishing AI-assisted content:

- [ ] All facts verified
- [ ] Brand voice consistent
- [ ] Expert insights added
- [ ] SEO optimized
- [ ] Unique value clear
- [ ] No AI artifacts (weird phrases)
