# MartAI Competitor Analysis & Value Proposition

## Executive Summary

After analyzing SEMrush (enterprise SEO platform) and Helps2 (Kansas City marketing agency), MartAI has a unique opportunity to fill the gap between these two extremes: **an AI-powered SEO platform that feels like having your own marketing strategist, at a fraction of the cost**.

---

## Competitor Profiles

### SEMrush

**Type**: Enterprise SaaS Platform
**Pricing**: $130-$500+/month
**Target**: Agencies, marketers, enterprises

| Strengths              | Weaknesses                     |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| 26B+ keyword database  | Overwhelming UI for beginners  |
| Comprehensive toolset  | Expensive ($1,560-$6,000/year) |
| AI Visibility tracking | Requires SEO expertise to use  |
| Trusted brand          | No human guidance              |
| Enterprise-ready       | Data overload without action   |

**Key Features:**

- Keyword Magic Tool (billions of keywords)
- Competitor analysis
- Site audit
- Backlink analysis
- Position tracking
- Content marketing toolkit
- AI Visibility (tracks brand in ChatGPT, Perplexity)
- AI PR toolkit

**Pain Points for Small Business:**

- Too expensive for solopreneurs/SMBs
- Steep learning curve
- Shows data but doesn't tell you what to DO
- No content creation, just research

---

### Helps2

**Type**: Full-Service Marketing Agency
**Pricing**: $1,500-$5,000+/month (estimated)
**Target**: Small businesses, nonprofits, Kansas City local

| Strengths              | Weaknesses               |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Human expertise        | Very expensive           |
| Custom strategy        | Slow turnaround          |
| Local market knowledge | Doesn't scale            |
| Relationship-focused   | Limited by team capacity |
| Done-for-you service   | No self-service option   |

**Services:**

- Website design
- Brand identity
- SEO services
- Social media management
- Paid ads (Google/Meta)
- Local SEO

**Pain Points:**

- Too expensive for startups/solopreneurs
- Waiting for deliverables
- No transparency into strategy
- Locked into monthly retainers
- Hard to scale up/down
